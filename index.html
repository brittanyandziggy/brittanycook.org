About

Hello, I'm Brittany.

I'm a research assistant based in Melbourne at Orygen Youth Mental Health, passionate about early psychosis, cognition, novel interventions and neuroimaging.

I'm currently lucky enough to work across the CogScreen study under Kelly Allott, Shayden Bryce and Alexandra Stainton. I also work to support Alexandra Stainton's pilot study investigating the relationship between coupled sleep oscillations and overnight memory consolidation in youth depression.

Prior to my current role, I completed my studies at The University of Auckland in New Zealand, graduating with a Bachelor of Advanced Science with first class honours, majoring in Psychology and Neuroscience.
